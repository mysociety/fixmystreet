Selecting categories for: [% label_for_field(form, 'parish', form.saved_data.parish) %]

The next step is to pick what things you would like to receive reports about.

Perhaps you are responsible for all grass cutting within your area, in which case you could add a “Grass cutting” category. Or perhaps you look after a particular playing field, so a “Lifford Lane playing field” category could work for that.

We understand each parish/town council is different, and you have the freedom to pick what categories work for you.

If you have devolved responsibilities

Let’s say you look after all the road signs, but there already is a road signs category - then you can have a category of the same name, and within your boundary you will take precedence and receive reports in that category.

If you are only responsible for some of the road signs, you could choose a category name that makes it clearer to the user which category they should pick, like “Road signs on the estate roads”. See some examples:

  • Bridgwater Town Council in Somerset is responsible for a wide array of things within their area – their categories include Dog fouling, Flytipping, Graffiti, Grass cutting, Litter/dog bins, Parks, Street lighting in parks, Trees in parks (not roads), and Weed growth.
  • Adstock Parish Council in Buckinghamshire is responsible for Flyposting, Grass cutting, Hedge problem, Dirty signs and Unauthorised signs.
