Press resources

About SCC Incident Analysis

SCC Incident Analysis is an independent web service run by the charity mySociety to make it easier for anyone to report a problem in their local area without needing to have any knowledge of council boundaries or responsibilities.

You can find the answers to many of our most frequently asked questions on our FAQ page.

Using SCC Incident Analysis data in your story

We welcome enquiries about and support the use of SCC Incident Analysis data for constructive research purposes.

Please note, mySociety does not endorse the simplistic use of SCC Incident Analysis data to compare, denounce or rank areas.

In most cases, data from SCC Incident Analysis alone cannot be used to make fair comparisons at local or national levels. Learn about what SCC Incident Analysis report data can and can’t tell you.

How to contact us

We are a small, fully remote organisation, so the best way to reach us is to email press@mysociety.org and one of the team will respond to you as quickly as possible.

Please try to give us as much advance warning as possible for requests to provide comments, interviews or statistics. As a charity, we operate very leanly and with limited resources, so the more time you give us to support your request the better.

Please note, we can’t respond to queries about reports made on SCC Incident Analysis.

  • If your question is a technical one, please visit the SCC Incident Analysis user support page.
  • If you are following up on the status of a report, please contact the council or authority it was sent to directly (we only send reports to the people who can fix them; we can’t control what happens afterwards).
